AI Sports

NBA

ClutchCast AI

Predicts an NBA team's chance of winning at every moment of a live game. The platform turns raw play-by-play data into 85 predictive signals, tests four different models against each other on games none of them have seen before, and automatically promotes the most accurate one to make live predictions.

Stack
Python, pandas, scikit-learn, PyTorch, Streamlit, Plotly, Flask, SocketIO, nba_api
Evaluation
Four models (rule-based baseline, logistic regression, random forest, PyTorch neural net) ranked by Brier score, log-loss, ROC-AUC, and accuracy on 28,463 plays across 60 games held out from training, drawn from a 300-game season sample
Result
The neural network won: 71.5% accuracy on games it had never seen, with the strongest probability calibration of the four (0.180 Brier score, 0.81 ROC-AUC)

NVIDIA Spark Hackathon

NVIDIA City of Toronto

Delation

Emergency-response prototype built at the NVIDIA Spark Hackathon in Toronto. Incident reports arrive as voice, video, and location; the system layers them with city data, flood maps, and live weather to flag which neighbourhoods are most at risk, all running locally with no cloud required.

Stack
Python, FastAPI, Uvicorn, Pydantic, Faster-Whisper, Kokoro ONNX, WebSockets, Next.js, TypeScript, Mapbox GL JS
Role
One of five engineers on the team. Built the FastAPI gateway, the system's front door: it receives every incident report, converts speech to text and back, streams live status updates, and merges Toronto Open Data, floodplain maps, and Environment Canada weather into a risk score for each neighbourhood.
Outcome
Shipped a fully working prototype inside the 36-hour hackathon window, built to analyze an incoming incident in under a tenth of a second on the team's on-site NVIDIA hardware.
